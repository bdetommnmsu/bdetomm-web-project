Places I've Visited

Over the years I've had the privilege of visiting some truly remarkable places. Alaska blew me away with its sheer scale - massive glaciers, dense forests, and wildlife you just can't see anywhere else in the lower 48. Standing in Denali National Park with no roads, no crowds, and mountains stretching as far as the eye can see is something I'll never forget.

Victoria, British Columbia is one of the most charming cities in North America. The British colonial architecture, the famous Butchart Gardens, and the friendly locals make it feel like a little piece of England tucked into the Pacific Northwest.

The Caribbean was pure paradise - turquoise water, white sand beaches, and some of the freshest seafood I've ever had. And Hong Kong was unlike anything else: a jaw-dropping skyline, incredibly efficient public transit, amazing dim sum, and a culture that perfectly blends East and West.

My Dream Destinations

Italy is at the very top of my list. From the ancient Colosseum in Rome to the canals of Venice and the rolling hills of Tuscany, Italy feels like an open-air museum. The food alone - authentic pasta, wood-fired pizza, fresh gelato - would make the trip worth it.

Japan is the other destination I keep coming back to. The contrast between the ultra-modern cities like Tokyo and the ancient temples of Kyoto is fascinating. I'd love to visit during cherry blossom season and see Mount Fuji up close.

  1. Italy - Rome, Florence, Venice, Tuscany, Amalfi Coast
  2. Japan - Tokyo, Kyoto, Osaka, Mount Fuji, Hiroshima

My Personal Travel Tips

  • Always buy travel insurance - it's saved me more than once
  • Learn a few words in the local language - locals always appreciate the effort
  • Pack light and leave room in your bag for souvenirs
  • Get off the tourist trail and explore local neighborhoods
  • Try the street food - it's usually the most authentic and delicious option
  • Keep digital and physical copies of all important documents
  • Download offline maps before you arrive (Google Maps works great)
